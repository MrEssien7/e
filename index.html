I'm a third-year BComm Honours Business Administration and Computer Science Co-op student at the University of Windsor. The pairing isn't incidental: most organizational problems are data problems wearing a different name, and most data problems only make sense once you understand the business they're feeding. Studying both at once means building toward sitting in that overlap on purpose.

Right now I'm on co-op as a Data & Reporting Analyst in the Office of the AVP External Relations, building field-level data dictionaries across five enterprise systems including Salesforce and Raiser's Edge. The work is mapping how information actually moves through an institution: where it's entered, who owns it, where it breaks down. It's taught me how to take an ambiguous problem, interview the people who live inside it, and turn what they know into something structured enough for someone else to use.

Outside of that, I'm Strategy Lead at UWindsor BSCS and wrote its 56-page organizational blueprint, because the same instinct applies: build something structured enough to outlast the person who started it. The throughline across all of it is the same: take something messy, make it legible, and leave it better than you found it.
